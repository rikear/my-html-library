Here, top and left are positioning the inner box inside its container so that the top-left corner of the inner box starts at a point 50% along and 50% down the outer box. Then, transform is working on the inner element and positioning it negatively in those axes by half (- 50%) of its own wiath and height. Nice!

One aspect of what we learned here bears repeating. When using percentages in transforms, remember that they are a percentage of the element being transformed, not its parent.
