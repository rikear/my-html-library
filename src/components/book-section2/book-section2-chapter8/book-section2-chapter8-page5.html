Background gradients

In days gone by, to achieve a background gradient on an element, it was necessary to tile a thin, graphical slice of the gradient. It was a pain to tweak as it meant round trips into a graphics ap-plication, and then when a site was live, you would often experience a flash of unloaded gradient while the background image was fetched.

Thankfully, such hassle is now nothing more than a memory; with a CSS background- image gradi-ent, things are far more flexible. CSS now enables us to create linear, radial, and conic background gradients, and repeating versions of each. Let's look at how we can define them.
