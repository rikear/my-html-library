Animating clip-path

As if all this clipping malarkey wasn't interesting enough, you can also animate a clip-path, as long as there remains the same number of points in the shape. For example, you can animate a triangle into a different shaped triangle, but without some tricks, you can't convert that same triangle into a star. I say "without some tricks" because you can effectively do this by hiding a few points along the lines of the polygon so your triangle has enough points to animate into a star.

You may be wondering, if we wanted to tint the image with a color, why don't we just use a background color in the declaration?

The answer is that it doesn't work. When you set multiple backgrounds with the background property, it's not possible to use a background-color, even a semi-transparent one, above an image, and have the color let the image show through beneath.
